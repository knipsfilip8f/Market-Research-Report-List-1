<p><p>The air-electrode battery market primarily encompasses zinc-air and lithium-air batteries. Zinc-air batteries utilize zinc as the anode and oxygen from the air as the cathode, offering high energy density and cost-effectiveness, making them ideal for applications like hearing aids and electric vehicles. In contrast, lithium-air batteries harness lithium as the anode and oxygen from the environment, promising exceptionally high energy density but facing challenges, including limited cycle life and stability. Both types present significant potential for energy storage innovations.</p></p>
