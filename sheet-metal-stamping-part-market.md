<p><p>The sheet metal stamping part market is segmented into several types, including blanking, embossing, bending, coining, and flanging. Blanking involves cutting out flat shapes from sheet metal. Embossing creates raised or recessed designs. Bending changes the metal's shape at specific angles. Coining compresses the metal into a defined shape with high precision. Flanging involves bending the edge of the sheet to create a flange for assembly. Other techniques may include deeper drawing, perforating, and notching, enhancing design versatility.</p></p>
